Plan Gets Some New Flourishes

State Street Redesign

Wisconsin State Journal :: FRONT :: A1

Friday, August 29, 2003
Lesley Rogers Barrett County reporter

State Street's proposed $15 million facelift is getting more nips and tucks, including a circular logo to help market Madison's most famous street.

Light poles with color-coded banners identifying each block of State Street, colorful mosaics at the intersections and a grander Capitol entrance are part of the latest plans to revamp the showcase street that runs from the Capitol to UW-Madison.

The designs and the proposed logo - a simple circle that says "State Street" - will be presented at a public meeting Sept. 11. The final concept still needs approval from the Madison City Council.

"State Street is the most important street in the city, and I'm very excited about what's been presented," said Ken Opin, chairman of the State Street Design Project Oversight Committee, which met Thursday.


The committee and a new set of engineers and designers are making changes to a conceptual design that was approved last year by the City Council.

One change includes saving many of the trees along State Street, instead of chopping them down.

Another feature is a logo, to help brand the funky street of restaurants and shops.

Mark Schmitz of ZD Studios, who designed the logo, said it's not meant to be an icon representing all of State Street.

"It's a graphic thumbprint," Schmitz said.

It's likely to be used small, on banners, signs and even on tree grates, he said.

The latest designs include a plaza-like space at the start of State Street near the Capitol, with a raised pavement and no curbs, said Ken Saiki, a Madison landscape architect.

"It should expand the event space, for the Farmer's Market or Jazz at Five," Saiki said.

Potted plants and trees will help separate the pedestrian area from the street, he said.

Bus shelters will be smaller and use more glass. Public art will be incorporated throughout the street. And street lamps will include a banner system to help people identify with each block of State Street.

"I think the average person doesn't have each block face memorized, if something is the 200 or 400 block," City Council President Mike Verveer said. "Signage has been less than impressive in the past."

There will also be directional signs, pointing to places of interest, such as the Madison Children's Museum, UW-Madison and the future Overture Center.

"This is the first I've seen these new designs and I'm very impressed," Verveer said. "I'm anxious to get reaction from the public."

A final design is expected to be approved by the end of this year, with construction to start on the 100 block of State Street next year. The 200 block -- the site of the Overture Center -- will follow in 2005.

City planner Bill Fruhling said the timeline for the rest of the State Street hasn't been determined.

He also said last year's price tag of $15 million is only an estimate, and it will probably cost less when the plans are finalized.
