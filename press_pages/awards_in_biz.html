Awards in Business

From the Journal Sentinel
Posted: Nov. 7, 2004

Health care

The DREAM Award was given to Medical Associates' Coumadin Clinic, a comprehensive health management program for the prevention of heart attacks, strokes and blockage of major arteries.

Printing

Wisconsin Trails Magazine won five writing awards at the 2004 International Regional Magazine Association Conference. They are Historical Feature Silver Award: "The Queens of Racine," Perry M. Lamek; Nature Feature Silver Award: "Symbol of the Lake Country," Tom Davis; General Feature Silver Award: "Return to the Shack," Erik Ness; Profiles Bronze Award: "The Chronicler of Nobbern," Tom Davis; and Essay Award of Merit: "To the Cottage," Eddie Allen.

Professional services

Richard Atchison, a SEEK Careers / Staffing employee, has been named the Wisconsin Staffing Employee of the Year by the Wisconsin Association of Staffing Services.

Technology

ZD Studios, Madison, won four international awards in the Creativity 34 design competition, for Web site designs for Westin Kierland Resort in Scottsdale, Ariz., for Ken Saiki Design and Groth Design Group, Madison. It also won a facility brand design award for Miller Brewing Companies Visitor Tour program.

Transportation

Wal-Mart Stores Inc. recognized Schneider National, Green Bay, as the recipient of two of the Bentonville, Ark., retailer's Carrier of the Year awards. Schneider National also was named a recipient of the 2004 CIO 100 award by CIO magazine. The awards recognize companies that exemplify operational and strategic excellence in information technology.

Miscellaneous

Focus on Energy, Madison, received an Inspiring Efficiency Education award from the Midwest Energy Efficiency Alliance.

Wisconsin Energy Conservation Corp. has been honored by the American Council for an Energy-Efficient Economy as a 2004 Champion of Energy Efficiency.

Sargento Food Inc., Plymouth, received the Deloitte & Touche Greater Good Award.

The Milwaukee Journal Sentinel publishes awards and achievements of significant public interest earned by companies and individuals in Wisconsin. The newspaper reserves the right to not publish certain types of awards and not all awards submitted for publication will be published. For consideration, send awards to Business Desk, Milwaukee Journal Sentinel, P.O. Box 371, Milwaukee, WI 53201-0371
