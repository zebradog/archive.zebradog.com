Camp Randall Hall Of Fame Proposed\

Wisconsin State Journal :: BUSINESS :: D10

Tuesday, December 2, 2003
Jason Stein Business reporter

A local designer and the UW-Madison Athletic Department want to use the renovation of Camp Randall to put Badger fans in touch with the football stadium's past.

Mark Schmitz, the head of Madison's ZD Studios, said he's talking with university officials about designing a mini hall of fame and other exhibits for the ground level of the stadium's east side.

The exhibits would seek to make Camp Randall a year-round destination by laying out its long history as a sports field and its beginnings as a training camp for Civil War-era troops, Schmitz said.

Vince Sweeney, senior associate athletic director, said the project's fate depended largely on money.


"We'd like to have it when the facility is complete in 2005, but even if we don't it's still something we'd like to incorporate in the near future," Sweeney said.

Schmitz has already designed signs and graphics for Camp Randall's $84 million renovation, including a newly unveiled logo. The design incorporates the "W" crest found on the nearby Field House and has lettering and scrollwork that match the old university symbol.

Schmitz, who grew up watching games and selling sodas at Camp Randall, brings the enthusiasm of a longtime Badger fan to the project.

"I've spent the last two months reading history books and old yearbooks to find out what happened there in order to tell an accurate story," Schmitz said.

ZD Studios, which also designed the new logo for Lambeau Field in Green Bay, has been working for eight months on the Camp Randall project, which will bring the firm about $75,000. As part of the State Street redesign team, its designers have also spent more than two years working on a $70,000 contract to create a logo and graphics for the street.

The contracts are a small part of the revenues at ZD Studios, which has 15 employees and took in around $2 million in sales in 2002.

But for Schmitz, the work is a welcome chance to revisit the days he spent watching UW running back Billy Marek at Camp Randall or living in an apartment above State Street.

"It's part of me. It's part of my fabric," he said.
